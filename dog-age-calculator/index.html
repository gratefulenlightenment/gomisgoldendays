How the estimate works

Dog years are not a simple multiplication.

Dogs mature much faster than people during their first two years. After that, the pace varies with adult size: larger dogs generally move through later life stages sooner than smaller dogs.

This calculator uses your dog’s exact age in years and months, then estimates a human-equivalent age from a size-based physiological age chart. It is a helpful way to think about life stage—not a diagnosis or a prediction of lifespan.

Frequently asked questions

About dog age in human years

Is one dog year equal to seven human years?

Not exactly. Dogs mature quickly in their first years, and their later aging rate varies with adult size. This calculator uses a size-aware age chart rather than multiplying every year by seven.

Why does adult size matter?

Dogs of different adult sizes tend to age at different rates, especially later in life. Large and giant dogs generally reach senior life stages earlier than smaller dogs.

Is this result exact?

No. It is a playful estimate based on a physiological age chart. Breed, genetics, health, and lifestyle also affect aging, so the result is not veterinary advice.
