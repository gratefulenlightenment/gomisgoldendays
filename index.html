Gomi's Story

When ordinary days became golden

Gomi was full of energy from the very start. As she grew, she became something of a giant among Yorkies — a solid 11 pounds (5kg) — so bursting with life we called her Gomi the Super Yorkie.

I photographed everything. Instagram, YouTube, all of it — I couldn't get enough of her.

Then, somewhere along the way, she just… became part of the everyday. The camera came out less. The posts got quieter. She was still there, still mine, but I'd stopped really seeing her.

When Gomi turned 11, everything changed. A serious surgery, with the risk of complications. A stubborn eye infection that simply wouldn't go away. A long, difficult recovery.

Somewhere in those weeks and months, it hit me — she could actually disappear from my life. And suddenly, every small thing she did felt achingly precious. Things I'd stopped noticing became things I couldn't look away from.

That's when I knew: the time we have left is golden, and I want to record every bit of Gomi — who is still the Super Yorkie, even if she rests a little more often now and limps from time to time.

So I picked the camera back up. And I started making this journal — not just for me, but to share these golden days with everyone who loves Gomi, and everyone loving a dog of their own.

The Journal

Not a diary you have to fill in perfectly.

Some days you'll write a full page. Some days, just one small joy. Both are enough. Ninety days of gentle prompts to hold onto the little moments — and quietly notice the small changes that are easy to miss.

  • Golden Moments — catch the little joys before they slip away
  • Tiny Changes — turn a vague worry into something you can see
  • Care & Vet Notes — the practical pages to bring with you
  • Love & Memory — a letter to the dog you love, out loud

Print it at home, or use it digitally on your iPad or tablet (GoodNotes, Notability, and similar apps). However you keep it, there's no wrong way to remember a dog you love.
